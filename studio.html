The clever bit

Light, shaped by thickness.

  • Your image becomes a relief — thicker where it should be dark, thinner where it should be bright.
  • With a light behind it, thin areas glow and thick areas stay shadowed, recreating the photo in light.
  • Get the source image right and the detail is striking — which is why we start from the highest-resolution photo you have.
  • Classic pieces look best in white or warm-white for the cleanest contrast — pick crisp black-and-white or a vintage sepia tone, or go full-colour with a CMYK layering technique.
